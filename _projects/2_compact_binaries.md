My research studies the formation and evolution of compact-object binaries, with particular emphasis on binary neutron stars and binary black holes.

The main topics include:

- common-envelope evolution;
- binary neutron-star formation;
- natal kicks and supernova mechanisms;
- metallicity and cosmic star-formation history;
- compact-binary merger-rate evolution;
- eccentric compact binaries;
- joint constraints from Galactic binaries and gravitational-wave observations.

This work combines binary population synthesis, pulsar observations, gravitational-wave measurements, and statistical inference.
